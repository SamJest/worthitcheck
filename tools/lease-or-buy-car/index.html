      How it works

      Simple car ownership tradeoffs, weighted like a real decision

      Timeline matters first

      Shorter timelines often favor leasing. Longer timelines usually make buying more attractive.

      Mileage changes everything

      Higher annual mileage makes lease limits harder to live with and is one of the strongest reasons to buy instead.

      Payments vs ownership

      Lower monthly payments support leasing, while long-term ownership value supports buying.

      Lease vs buy

      When should you lease vs buy a car

      If you are asking whether to lease or buy a car, the most important signals are how long you expect to keep it, how much you drive, whether lower monthly payments matter more than ownership, and how comfortable you are with mileage and wear limits. This tool gives a practical heuristic answer, not a dealer-style finance worksheet.

      FAQ

      Common lease vs buy car questions

      Is it better to lease or buy a car for a short time?

      Often lease, especially if lower monthly payments and flexibility matter more than long-term ownership value.

      Does high mileage make buying better than leasing?

      Usually yes. Higher mileage can make lease limits harder to live with and pushes the decision toward buying.

      When does buying usually beat leasing?

      Buying usually becomes stronger when you plan to keep the car for many years, drive a lot, care about ownership, or dislike lease restrictions.

      Why do lower monthly payments point toward leasing?

      Lower monthly cost is one of leasing's biggest practical advantages, especially when long-term ownership is not a major priority.

      Can lease vs buy be a borderline decision?

      Yes. Mid-length timelines with mixed payment and ownership goals often produce a close call that is not one-sided.

      Is this a full lease vs buy calculator?

      No. WorthItCheck gives a practical heuristic recommendation rather than a full finance calculation with APR, taxes, or dealer-specific terms.
