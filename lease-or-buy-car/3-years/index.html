Lease or buy a car for 3 years?

A 3-year timeline often sits right on the line where lease flexibility starts to look attractive but buying can still win if the costs and end value stay strong.

3-year horizonUpfront cost recoveryMileage fitEnd equity potential

Quick answer

What usually makes sense

If you are fairly sure you only want the car for about three years, leasing often becomes easier to justify. Buying gets stronger when mileage stays sensible, finance is competitive, and the expected end value still leaves you with enough equity to matter.

What usually matters most

  • Short ownership windows usually favour flexibility more than long-term value building.
  • Buying only gets stronger when the residual value still leaves enough equity after finance pressure.
  • Higher mileage or weak resale expectations can quickly break the short-term buy case.
  • A real lease quote and mileage cap matter more here than vague averages.

FAQ

Common questions

Is leasing usually better if I only want the car for about 3 years?

Often yes. A 3-year horizon gives leasing more room to make sense because the flexibility stays valuable and the buy side has less time to recover its upfront cost.

Can buying still win over 3 years?

Yes, especially when mileage is moderate, finance is reasonable, and you expect the car to hold enough value for equity to matter.

What should I verify on a close call?

Verify the real lease quote, upfront rental, mileage cap, APR, and likely resale value before acting on a close result.
