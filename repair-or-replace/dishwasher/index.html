Repair or replace a dishwasher?

Dishwasher repairs are easier to justify when the unit is mid-life and the repair solves a single known fault. Older machines with leak risk or repeated breakdowns often push more strongly toward replacement.

Leak risk Age pressure Repair share Repeat failure risk

Quick answer

What usually makes sense

If the dishwasher is not very old and the repair is a targeted fix, repair can still be good value. Expensive repairs late in life are much harder to justify, especially when water damage risk enters the picture.

One repair is okay

A pump, latch, or sensor issue on a mid-life dishwasher can still be worth fixing if the rest of the machine is solid.

Leaks raise the stakes

Water damage risk makes repeated or uncertain repairs less attractive than they would be on lower-risk items.

Older units flip faster

Once the dishwasher is deep into its expected lifespan, even moderate repairs start to look weaker.

Replacement value matters

If a decent replacement is not dramatically more expensive than the repair, replacing often becomes easier to defend.

FAQ

Common dishwasher repair questions

Is it worth fixing an older dishwasher?

Sometimes, but only if the repair is modest and the machine has been otherwise reliable. Older units become harder to justify because more failures often follow.

Should I replace a leaking dishwasher?

Often yes if the dishwasher is already older or has more than one problem. Leak risk makes uncertain repairs less attractive.

What dishwasher repairs are most worth doing?

Lower-cost single-part fixes like latches, pumps, or simple sensors are usually the easiest repairs to defend on mid-life machines.
