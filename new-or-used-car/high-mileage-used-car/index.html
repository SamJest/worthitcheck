Should I buy a used car with high mileage?

A high-mileage used car can still be good value, but only when the price gap is large enough to compensate for the extra maintenance, inspection, and history risk you are taking on.

Mileage riskPrice discountInspection qualityMaintenance tolerance

Quick answer

What usually makes sense

A high-mileage used car is easiest to justify when the discount is strong, the history is clear, and you are comfortable with more wear-related risk. If the price gap is thin or reliability certainty matters a lot, new or lower-mileage used options become easier to defend.

What usually matters most

  • The discount has to be real enough to offset higher wear risk.
  • Inspection and service history matter more here than in a normal used-car comparison.
  • If surprise repairs would hurt badly, the cheap price may not be enough on its own.
  • Mileage by itself is not the whole story, but it raises the burden of proof.

FAQ

Common questions

Can a high-mileage used car still be worth buying?

Yes, but only when the price discount is real enough to compensate for the higher maintenance and history risk. Inspection quality matters a lot here.

When does a high-mileage used car become too risky?

It becomes much harder to justify when the discount is thin, records are weak, or you are very sensitive to surprise repair costs.

What should I verify next?

Verify the service history, inspection findings, tyres, brakes, known fault patterns, and the realistic price gap to lower-mileage alternatives.
